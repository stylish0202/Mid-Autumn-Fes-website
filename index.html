Festival Origin Story

The Mid-Autumn Festival is a traditional celebration that began in ancient China over 2,000 years ago. It started as a way to give thanks for the harvest and to admire the full moon, which symbolized unity and abundance. Over time, it became closely linked with famous legends like the story of Chang'e, the Moon Goddess, and the Jade Rabbit. Today, people celebrate by gathering with family, enjoying mooncakes, and lighting lanterns.

One of the most beautiful stories I came across while learning about the Mid-Autumn Festival is the legend of Chang'e. She was the wife of a brave archer named Hou Yi, who saved the world by shooting down nine suns. As a reward, he received an elixir of immortality. But when a thief tried to steal it, Chang'e drank the elixir to keep it safe, and as a result, she floated up to the moon, where she still lives today.

Families gather under the moonlight and set up altars with mooncakes and tea to give thanks for a bountiful harvest and to wish for harmony, luck, and reunion.

The Jade Rabbit is said to live on the moon, making elixirs of immortality. Some say you can still see its shape on the full moon.
